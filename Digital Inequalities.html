The Manifestation And Negative Impacts Of Digital Inequality On Social Media

This blog aims to discuss how digital inequalities perform and influence female users on social platforms. For digital inequalities, it can be defined as the social status that affects their digital access, skills, and usage patterns, as well as the results of engagements (Buchi and Hargittai, 2022, p.1). It leads to unfair treatment and prejudice against women when using internet, ‘Instagram’ will be an example on this page.

Digital Inequalities Digital Media and Collective Action Digital Well-Being

  • Women have a high risk of being maliciously harmed by strangers when using a social platform:

Internet harassment: People send messages of sexual harassment to female users by observing their ins pages and profile just because they do not think female users have high status online. The factors that people harass women on the Internet can be attributed to technological factors, an immature legal system, and anomalous individuals (Banet-Weiser and Miltner, 2016)

  • The appearance anxiety of female users caused by Instagram filters:

Most of the filters are made for girls to make girls’ facial features into the beautiful appearance expected by society instead of showing the original look. However, there are almost no special filters designed for male users which is just because society always forces women to be more perfect to achieve their high expectations.

  • Conclusion:

In the digital society, digital inequalities have deepened the prejudice against female users on social platforms. It puts females in a disadvantaged position and greatly hindered women’s process in developing their digital skills and obliterates their rights to freely use the internet. People should pay attention to the character of the atrocity for women, especially the repeated allegations of falsification against women in the digital society (Duffy, Miltner and Wahlstedt, 2022). Finally, be inclusive of women to create a more orderly and healthy digital society.
