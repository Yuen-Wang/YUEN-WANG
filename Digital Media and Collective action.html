Collective Action Helps People Develop a Sense Of Social Responsibility By Digital Media

This Blog is dedicated to explaining how people use digital media to engage in collective action and build a sense of social responsibility. Moreover, due to the widespread of collective action, has brought good benefits to some special groups of society. I will use WakeUpCall as a case on this page for analysis.

Digital Inequalities Digital Media and Collective Action Digital Well-Being

Definition of Collective Action: it means an emphasis on having individuals contribute to collective efforts that often involve looking for some kind of public good (Bennett and Segerberg, 2012)

WakeUpCall was launched by a UNICEF ambassador to get donations to UNICEF to help the children affected by the war in Syria. The way to take part in this challenge is for people to post a selfie of themselves waking up in bed with #WAKEUPCALL and #UNICEF. Every participant needs to donate five pounds to UNICEF and then mention three other people to do the same in the post. One of the reasons for the success of the campaign, which raised 7 million dollars, is the sense of social responsibility that comes from people in digital media. Because a person may feel responsible when their contributions can significantly influence the welfare of the group (Fleishman, J. A., 1980), and they will feel satisfied and proud that they joined. Therefore, when collective action appears on digital media, people are willing to join the challenge, it not only builds their sense of social responsibility but also helps some vulnerable groups.

  • Conlusion:

To sum up, collective action fosters a sense of responsibility in people and is reflected in digital media with positive participation because social media is always chosen by NGOs to disseminate their message to potential global audiences. Therefore, more people will join collective action to create public benefits and social responsibilities in the digital society.
